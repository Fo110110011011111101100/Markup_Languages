Mercedes-Benz

Mercedes-Benz Group AG ([mɛʁˈtseːdəs ˈbɛnts ɡɹuːp],
formerly Daimler AG, DaimlerChrysler AG, Daimler-Benz AG) is a multinational automotive concern
headquartered in Stuttgart (Baden-Württemberg), Germany. The largest company in Germany in terms of
turnover (according to the results of 2006). The concern was founded as Daimler-Benz AG in 1926 as
a result of the merger of two companies: Daimler Motoren Gesellschaft and Benz & Cie. In 1998, the
company bought out the Chrysler Group and changed its name to DaimlerChrysler AG. After the resale
of Chrysler shares to the investment fund Cerberus Capital Management, the concern was renamed again,
this time as Daimler AG. In February 2022, Daimler AG was renamed Mercedes-Benz Group AG.

Audi

Audi AG ([ˈɑʊdɪ], audi in Latin - “listen”) is a German
automotive company within the Volkswagen Group, specializing in the production of cars under the Audi brand.
The headquarters is located in the city of Ingolstadt (Germany). The motto is Vorsprung durch Technik
(from German - "Progress through technology"). The production volume in 2016 was about 1,903,259 vehicles.

BMW

BMW AG (Russian Be-M-Ve, an abbreviation for Bayerische Motoren
Werke AG, with German - "Bavarian Motor Works") is a German manufacturer of cars, motorcycles, engines,
and bicycles. From 2006 to 2015, Norbert Reithofer was the chairman of the company, since May 2015, Harald Krüger,
and since July 18, 2019, Oliver Zipse. The chief designer is Josef Kaban. The motto of the company is
"Freude am Fahren", with it. — "Happy driving." For English-speaking countries, the motto "The Ultimate
Driving Machine" was also coined (from English - "The Ideal Driving Machine").
